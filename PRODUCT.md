# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Primary users are police officers, lost-and-found desk operators, volunteers, and control-room staff coordinating missing-person cases across Nashik–Trimbakeshwar during Kumbh Mela 2027. They work under time pressure, on shared screens and kiosks, with incomplete multilingual reports and uneven connectivity. Families and pilgrims use an app-free intake and a no-login status page to report a missing person and understand what is happening without exposing personal information.

## Product Purpose

Setu closes the operational gap between independently run lost-and-found centers. It normalizes sparse multilingual reports, geo-resolves last-seen locations against existing infrastructure, surfaces cross-center found-person matches with explainable confidence, and keeps families informed. Success means operators can identify and approve a credible match quickly, the demonstration works without an API key or provisioned infrastructure, and the system presents a plausible path to government deployment at Kumbh scale.

## Brand Personality

Calm, authoritative, and humane. The operations console should communicate institutional competence without feeling militaristic; the pilgrim-facing surfaces should feel reassuring, plain-spoken, and trustworthy.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic sci-fi command centers, neon cyberpunk dashboards, decorative glass panels, consumer-social styling, and dense analytics that obscure the next operational action. Do not reuse the earlier problem-space explorer as the product identity. Do not present AI confidence as certainty or hide the human approval step.

## Design Principles

1. Put the next safe operational action ahead of decorative metrics.
2. Make cross-center visibility and explainable matching tangible in every core workflow.
3. Separate sensitive personal data from operational fields and reveal it only through explicit intent.
4. Degrade gracefully when connectivity or the model is unavailable; the operator must always retain a usable workflow.
5. Use calm, multilingual, low-literacy communication for families while preserving information density for trained operators.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Support keyboard operation, visible focus, reduced motion, color-independent status cues, readable contrast, touch targets suitable for kiosks, and responsive layouts. Pilgrim-facing content must avoid jargon, work without login, expose no PII, and leave clear seams for Indian-language localization, screen readers, and intermittent-network operation.
